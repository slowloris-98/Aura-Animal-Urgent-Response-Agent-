10 successful reports — domestic and wildlife — with the people who reported them and the rescuers who helped. Every story includes reporter actions and how they helped the rescue.

Case #1 · Dog High

Injured stray dog, Downey

Reported by Marcus Chen

His actions: Saw the dog limping near a parking lot, took a clear photo from a safe distance, used Aura to report with exact GPS and description “limping, possible front leg injury.” Stayed nearby and kept people from approaching until help arrived.

How it helped rescuers: Downey Animal Care Center used the photo and location to bring the right equipment and find the dog quickly. Marcus’s description helped them prepare for a possible fracture; the dog was safely captured and received treatment.

Rescued · Mar 2025

Case #2 · Cat Medium

Cat stuck in tree, Pasadena

Reported by Sarah Mitchell

Her actions: Noticed a cat about 15 feet up a tree, unable to get down. She reported via Aura with a photo and pin drop, noted “no visible injury, been there ~2 hours,” and waited at the base so the cat wouldn’t bolt when rescuers arrived.

How it helped rescuers: Pasadena Humane knew exactly which tree and that the cat was stable. Sarah’s presence helped keep the cat in place; the team used a ladder and carrier and brought the cat down safely. No injuries.

Rescued · Mar 2025

Case #3 · Dog Critical

Dog hit by car, Bell Gardens

Reported by David Park

His actions: Witnessed the collision, did not move the dog, and immediately reported via Aura with “unable to stand, bleeding from leg, conscious.” Shared live location and stayed to direct emergency responders.

How it helped rescuers: Miracle Dog Rescue and a local vet were dispatched with the right gear. David’s “don’t move” note and exact spot meant the team could stabilize the dog on-site and transport without further injury. Dog is now rehabilitating.

Rehabilitating · Mar 2025

Case #4 · Wildlife (Bird) High

Injured hawk, Pasadena

Reported by Elena Torres

Her actions: Found a large bird (hawk) on the ground, unable to fly, near a trail. She didn’t touch it, took a photo, and reported via Aura with “raptor, wing drooping, alert.” Stayed at a distance to discourage predators and people.

How it helped rescuers: Aura routed to a wildlife rehab partner. The photo and “raptor” description allowed them to bring proper gloves and a carrier. Elena’s vigil kept the bird in one place; it was captured, treated for a wing injury, and later released.

Rehabilitated & released · Feb 2025

Case #7 · Wildlife (Deer) Medium

Fawn alone near road, Glendale

Reported by Ryan O’Brien

His actions: Saw a small fawn alone next to a busy road. He did not touch it or approach closely, took a photo, and reported via Aura with “young deer, alone, no visible injury.” Waited from his car so the fawn wouldn’t bolt into traffic.

How it helped rescuers: Wildlife NGO was alerted; they knew it might be a “leave alone” case (mother nearby) or a true orphan. Ryan’s location and photo let them assess quickly. They monitored from a distance; when the mother didn’t return, they safely moved the fawn to a rehab facility.

Case #9 · Dog Critical

Dog in hot car, Bell Gardens

Reported by James Liu

His actions: Saw a dog panting heavily in a locked car on a 90°F day. He noted the make, color, and plate, reported via Aura as critical with “dog in car, windows up, no owner in sight,” and stayed to flag down security so they could locate the owner.

How it helped rescuers: Miracle Dog Rescue and local PD were dispatched. James’s vehicle description and location allowed quick arrival; the owner was paged and the dog was removed and cooled. The dog recovered fully; the incident was documented for follow-up.
